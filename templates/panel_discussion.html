DR BENJAMIN ANG

DR Benjamin Ang is a Senior Fellow with the Centre of Excellence for National Security (CNES) at the S. Rajaratnam School of International Studies (RSIS). He explores policies and legal issues in the cyber domain, including law enforcement technologies and cyber threats.

Prior to this, Dr Ang served as a litigation lawyer for commercial cases, IT Director, General Manager, corporate lawyer specialising in technology law, in-house legal counsel, Director-Asia in a regional technology consulting firm, in-house legal counsel in a transmedia company, and senior law lecturer at a local polytechnic specialising in data security and cybersecurity.

Dr Ang is also qualified as an Advocate and Solicitor of the Supreme Court of Singapore and as an Executive Committee member of the Internet Society Singapore Chapter, he also organises workshops and seminars on law and policy issues relating to internet use.

MS TEO YI-Ling

Ms Teo Yi-Ling is a Senior Fellow with the Centre of Excellence for National Security (CNES) at the S. Rajaratnam School of International Studies (RSIS). She explores policies and legal issues in the cyber domain, including law enforcement technologies and cyber threats.

Ms Teo has experience with international and local law firms in the areas of intellectual property, technology, media and entertainment. As a Senior Faculty and Principal Legal Counsel for the IP Academy at the Intellectual Property Office of Singapore, she led a team that developed and launched a specialist programme on intangible asset management.

She has extensive academic experiences as she has developed and taught courses in media law, intellectual property law, entertainment business transactions, and media ethics at a number of tertiary institutions in Singapore, and in the U.S., Dutch, and Australian university systems.

DR DIGANTA DAS

Dr Diganta Das is an Assistant Professor of Geography at the Humanities and Social Studies Education Academic Group, National Institute of Education, Nanyang Technological University.

Dr Das is a human geographer whose academic research interests focus on urban development in South Asia, especially on smart city development, urban policy mobility and producing high-tech spaces. He has published in the area of urban policy mobility, production of smart and high-tech spaces in India, and on changing urban waterscapes of South Asia. He is currently researching on Singapore’s Smart Nation initiatives and its impact on the quality of life of residents.

He was formerly the Chair of Regional Planning and Development Specialty Group (RDPSG) of American Association of Geographers (AAG), and is highly involved in urban and regional planning issues. He is active regionally around South and Southeast Asia in organising conferences through Southeast Asian Geography Association (SEAGA) where he is one of the executive members.

Dr MA SWAN HOO

Dr Ma Swan Hoo is a medical doctor who is involved in research on cosmetics. She also has a strong passion for history, and is a docent leader and mentor to fellow volunteers. She is highly sought after for her insights on making Singapore’s heritage relevant to national interests. In 2001, she completed docent training at the Singapore History Museum, now known as National Museum of Singapore. Since then, she has also guided visitors at the Peranakan Museum, IHC and National Gallery Singapore.

In 2004, Dr Ma helped to set up Museum Volunteers (MV), a group consisting of working professionals who guide visitors during weekends and public holidays. From 2011 to 2014, she was the President of MV, during which she worked closely with the volunteers and introduced an electronic scheduling system to achieve the nearly impossible task of keeping the no-show of docents close to zero.

DR FOO MIN LI

Dr Foo Min Li is an Assistant Director at the National Museum of Singapore (NMS). She heads the Programmes team which engages schools, families, members of the public and senior citizens at the museum. Prior to joining NMS, Min Li worked on the Research and Education portfolio at the Preservation of Sites and Monuments Division of the National Heritage Board from 2009 to 2016, researching on the national monuments’ history and presenting them through school and public programmes. Her current projects include working with the education team on a pilot programme to enhance students’ learning experience at the museum.

MS SITI NOOR MASTURA

MS Siti Noor Mastura's personal struggles with hunger and homelessness spurred her to start Back2Basics in 2013, a non-profit organisation that delivers free halal groceries to the doorsteps of beneficiaries such as home-bound elderly people and single mothers, the first such service for halal food delivery. Back2Basics is also committed to empowering youths from lower income families. In 2015, she co-founded Interfaith Youth Circle, an independent non-profit organisation that campaigns religious pluralism and interfaith cooperation.

As one who strongly champions gender equality, youth empowerment and civic responsibility through her training programs, speaking engagements and community partnerships, her efforts were recognised when she was conferred the 2016 President’s Volunteer and Philanthropy Award, the highest honour for giving to the community, by the President of Singapore. She was also awarded the Straits Times Singaporean of the Year in 2019 and she was recently recognised by Asia Tatler as one of Asia’s brightest young leaders, shaping and changing Singapore.

MR QIN YUNQUAN

Mr Qin Yunquan is the CEO and Co-founder of Kapap Academy, Singapore’s leading self-defence school. Yunquan is a Bio-engineering (Honours) graduate from National University of Singapore and a certified Behavioural Consultant in a personality assessment tool known as DISC.

Yunquan, co-founded an eclectic system of street style fighting, known as Modern Street Combatives, together with her teacher, Master Teo. Modern Street Combatives draws upon realistic self-defence systems such as KAPAP, Catch Wrestling, Gracie Combatives and Savate.

In recognition of Yunquan's contributions to society in helping ordinary people stay safe, she was personally conferred by Her Majesty, the Queen of England, the status of Queen's Young Leaders for Singapore (2017). This recognition makes her the only martial artist in the world ever to be awarded this prestigious accolade by the Queen herself under this award. Further to this recognition, Yunquan won a Citation Award as one of the top 10 finalists of the Singaporean of the Year Award (2017).

In 2018, Yunquan was recognised as an honouree of the Forbes' 30 Under 30 Asia - a list which features 300 young disruptors, innovators and entrepreneurs across Asia who are under the age of 30. She was also named by Singapore Tatler, Generation T, as one of the 50 most promising individuals in Singapore for 2018. The 150th year old Royal Commonwealth Society in 2018 conferred Yunquan the status of Associate Fellow with this international charity committed to youth empowerment, education and advocacy.

In 2019, Yunquan was one of 5 women selected around the world to be the brand ambassador for Converse, in their campaign, ‘All Her Stories Are True’. In the same year, Yunquan also became one of the finalists of the prestigious Women of the Future Awards under the Social Entrepreneur Category.

S. RAJARATNAM SCHOOL OF
INTERNATIONAL STUDIES

S. RAJARATNAM SCHOOL OF INTERNATIONAL STUDIES was officially inaugurated on 1 January 2007. Prior to this, it was known as the Institute of Defence and Strategic Studies (IDSS), which was established 10 years earlier, on 30 July 1996, by Dr Tony Tan Keng Yam, then Deputy Prime Minister and Minister for Defence. Like its predecessor, RSIS was established as an autonomous entity within Nanyang Technological University (NTU), with the goal of being a leading research and graduate teaching institution in strategic and international affairs in the Asia Pacific.

The school then expanded its research agenda and took on the name S. Rajaratnam School of International Studies (RSIS) in January 2007. The school was named after Mr S. Rajaratnam in recognition of his contributions to Singapore as its first Foreign Minister. Their schools aims to foster global networks and provide a platform for research in defence, national security, international relations, strategic studies and diplomacy.

NATIONAL INSTITUTE
OF EDUCATION

NATIONAL INSTITUTE OF EDUCATION is an autonomous institute of Nanyang Technological University in Singapore. Ranked 12th in the world and 2nd in Asia by the QS World University Rankings in the subject of Education in 2015, the institute is the sole teacher education institute for teachers in Singapore.

NIE established the Centre for Research in Pedagogy and Practice in 2002 with funding from the Ministry of Education (MOE). As the largest education research centre in the Asia Pacific, the centre sits in the Office of Educational Research. It brings together researchers, educators and administrators research in and to develop new and innovative ways of teaching and learning.

In January 2005, NIE established the Learning Sciences Lab which is now part of the Office of Educational Research. The Lab aims to lead and transform pedagogy enabled by ICT in teaching & learning in Singapore schools. From a research base, findings and good ideas are conceptualised and applied in learning processes so that these are effectively implemented in the schools.

NATIONAL HERITAGE BOARD

NATIONAL HERITAGE BOARD plays a key role in the development of a vibrant and diverse museum and heritage landscape in Singapore, through the cross-sharing of knowledge and expertise with fellow arts and heritage professionals. They endeavour to grow capabilities in the sector and encourage a culture of museum excellence, to lay a firm foundation for Singapore’s heritage that will guide into the future as well.

The festivals, language campaigns, heritage trails, education programmes and community grants they provide harnesses partnerships with the community and establishes long-standing relationships that allows Singapore to collectively recognise and celebrate our shared heritage.

MUSEUM VOLUNTEERS

MUSEUM VOLUNTEERS is a group of English-speaking volunteer guides. They provide free tours to the public in English at various National Heritage Board (NHB) museums and heritage institutions in Singapore. Their aim is to increase the public's appreciation of the collections in the museums and institutions as well as to enrich the experience of the visitors. In addition, some MVs volunteer with the Preservation of Sites and Monuments to offer tours of national monuments and with the Singapore Biennale to guide visitors when it is held. The group was also involved in the training of new guides for Singapore's very first community museum at Taman Jurong.

All MVs undergo training to familiarise themselves with the museums and institutions they guide at and to equip themselves with good communication and interpretive skills.They have about 200 members now. Most of the MVs are Singaporeans with the rest from Asia, Australia, Europe and North America. The age group ranges from the 20s to the 70s. Most of them hold full-time jobs and share a passion for heritage, culture and the arts.

NATIONAL MUSEUM SINGAPORE

With a history dating back to 1887, the NATIONAL MUSEUM SINGAPORE is the nation’s oldest museum with a progressive mind. Beneath its 19th-century colonial exterior, it uses cutting-edge technology to present the nation’s legacy and development. Its galleries highlight fresh perspectives of the Singapore story as they take you on an immersive voyage – one where creative storytelling redefines the conventional museum experience.

A cultural and architectural landmark in Singapore, the National Museum, hosts innovative activities and events all year round. These include the well-loved Night Festival, visually arresting art installations, as well as performances and film screenings of different styles and genres. You will also find a wide range of facilities and services such as a resource centre which has a growing collection of about 15,000 publications related to cultural studies, design and Singapore’s history.

NHB’s mission is to preserve and celebrate the shared heritage of our diverse communities, for the purpose of education, nation-building and cultural understanding. It manages the national museums and heritage institutions, and sets policies relating to heritage sites, monuments and the national collection. Through the national collection, NHB curates heritage programmes and presents exhibitions to connect the past, present and future generations of Singaporeans.

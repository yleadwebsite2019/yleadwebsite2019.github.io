INTRODUCTION
Singapore is a small country, located in Southeast Asia, and is commonly known as the “Little Red Dot”. It is a multi-racial country, with Chinese making up the bulk of the population, followed by Malays, Indians and Eurasians.
FOOD
Singapore is known to be a food paradise, offering food from many different parts of the world. Here are a few local dishes that we recommend trying!
Nasi Lemak is a Malay dish, which is popular among almost all locals. It is served with cucumbers, eggs, fish/chicken and many more. Its fragrant rice is cooked with coconut milk and pandan leaves.
Char Kway Teow is a Chinese dish, which is enjoyed by many locals. It is made from ricecake strips, served with simple ingredients. This dish can also be easily spotted in hawker centres!
Laksa is a spicy noodle soup, which is served with fishcake, egg, prawns and many more. With a creamy and spicy broth, it is one of Singaporeans' favourite local dishes.
Roti Prata is an Indian dish, which is commonly served with curry. It can be eaten plain, with egg, or even with cheese. It is definitely one of the top 5 local dishes we recommend you to try.
Rojak is a local salad of mixed vegetables, fruits, and dough fritters; covered in a sticky sweet sauce and garnished with chopped peanuts and finely-cut fragrant ginger flowers.

ICONIC LOCATIONS
MERLION
With the head of a lion and the body of a fish, the Merlion symbolizes both Singapore's humble beginnings as a fishing village and also the prospoerous 'Lion City' which we are today.
SINGAPORE BOTANIC GARDENS
As a UNESCO heritage site, the Botanic Gardens is the perfect spot to unwind and immerse yourself within the flora and fauna of Singapore, and is a must-see in Singapore!
GARDENS BY THE BAY
Enjoy a breath-taking waterfront view by taking a trip to Gardens by the Bay! Inspired by an orchid, the design resembles Singapore's National Flower, Vanda 'Miss Joaquim'. You can't miss the 9 to 16 storeys tall massive Supertrees here.
DOs & DON'Ts
DOs
  • Dress appropriately
  • Bring an umbrella
  • Bring a cap
  • Bring a power socket adaptor for Type G plug.
    Voltage is 230 V and the standard frequency is 50 Hz.
DON'Ts
    There are many things that are illegal under the law of Singapore that we have to take note of
  • Eat / Drink on public transport
  • Litter
  • Bring/Eat chewing gum
